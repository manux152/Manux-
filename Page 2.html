Overview

“Standing as a testament to human perseverance and architectural brilliance.”

The Great Wall of China is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, stretching over 13,000 miles. Built over centuries, it served as a defense system against invasions and raids.

Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of China’s strength and resilience.

Why Visit?

  • Breathtaking panoramic views
  • A hike through history
  • Unique cultural experience
  • Restored and preserved sections for visitors

Highlights & Points of Interest

  • Mutianyu: Less crowded, scenic views
  • Badaling: Most popular & accessible
  • Jinshanling: Picturesque, great for hiking
  • Simatai: Offers night tours
  • Huanghuacheng: Near a beautiful lake
